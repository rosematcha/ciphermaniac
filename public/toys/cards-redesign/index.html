/cards · redesign · round 2

Three refinements, all keeping the histogram.

Round one was too varied — too many different card patterns. This round shares one card design across three pages and varies the wrapper. Smaller cards, histogram restored on the art (warmer / more refined), no chin tile, tournament selector as a dropdown.

What changed from round 1
Histogram is back. Refined to gold instead of clinical blue, with softer gradient backing — keeps the visual identity without graffiti energy.
No chin. Stat tiles below cards are gone. Just a single label line: name on the left, set·number on the right.
Smaller cards. Grid is now 7 columns at desktop (was 5). Roughly twice as many cards visible per scroll.
Tournament = dropdown. No horizontal pill strip — handles 50+ events without scrolling.
No 7-day Δ. Trend indicators removed since per-day usage isn't wired up yet.
Sidebar gracefully degrades. Mockup A's sidebar collapses into a slide-out drawer on mobile.
The three mockups
A

The Binder — sidebar gallery

Persistent left rail with all filter controls visible at once: search, tournament dropdown, card type (with counts), set, archetype, regulation checkboxes, finish. Right side is just cards. Best for power users and repeat visitors who know what they're filtering by. On mobile the rail collapses into a drawer.

B

The Field Guide — editorial wrapper

A slim editorial intro: a serif headline, a lede, and a small "Defining the format" strip of the top five cards (rank badges, no extra chrome). Then the standard grid below with horizontal filter buttons. Best for newcomers and casual visitors — they get a story before the database.

C

The Playmat — warm & friendly

Same grid shape but with a warmer palette (deep brown/cream instead of grey/blue), rounded shapes, soft type chips in card corners (Pkmn / Item / Engy), and a personable "Hey — looking at the meta from…" greeting up top. Brand-forward without the tech-y feel.
